P3: Interaction Process Page

Puzzle Game

Since the interaction I was assigned was "drag", I browsed some examples related to drag, and was finally inspired by the picture of this simple puzzle game, then, I found a picture of a previous project and cut it into six equal parts. Initially I used "dragable" to move the position of different images. But after I added adaptive viewport to the page, I encountered the first problem: the adjusted images could not be arranged in the right place after the background size was changed. I tried many methods but to no avail. Finally, I found an alternative method when browsing "jqueryui.com/" - "sortable". In order to arrange the 6 images, I changed "display" to "grid", and now the pictures can finally be arranged even after the page size is changed. Also, I made the board draggable so user can adjust its position.

Dragable Button

I still needed an element that using "dragable", so I added a draggable button that can be clicked to show/hide the puzzle game. I used fonts found on adobe fonts.

Background

The background is composed of two parts stacked. At front is a canvas created through p5.js, and a rectangle of random size will appear constantly when the mouse is pressed. At back is a randomly generated small square background that I made based on the random position examples. A timer is used in js to loop through a function that changes the style of randomized squares every seconds.

I encountered some problems when setting elements as backgrounds, they always stack from top to bottom instead of front to back. After some research, I tried to integrate them into one element. By changing their z-index, order, and "position:relative" and "position:absolute", I finally made them overlap successfully.

Others

The sound effect materials are all from pixabay.com. I added a background music and a click sound effect to the button.
